All about food: Malaysia

Exploring food culture, prices, and eating habits through data visualisations.

Restaurant Ratings by State

This shows the average restaurant rating across several states in Malaysia. This gives us a general idea about where we can find the best food across the country. Food preference is a subjective matter, but this helps discover new and exciting restaurants.

TripAdvisor vs Google Reviews

However we must identify which is the most reliable platform to obtain restaurant reviews The bar chart below combines the total number of reviews on Trip Advisor and Google Reviews to find the most reviewed restaurants and compares their average ratings. We can observe that in general between these 10 restaurants, reviewers tend to rate restaurants slightly higher on Trip Advisor But the general rating for each restaurant is close to one another. So Trip Advisor or Google Reviews is a viable option to find good food.

Top Restaurants in Penang

From the map we can see that Penang has on average the highest rated restaurants on average. Below you can see the top 10 restaurants from Google Reviews.

Food Prices Over Time

The graph shows how Malaysia’s food producer prices shifted from 1991 to 2022, with sharp rises around 2008 and after 2020. These fluctuations highlight how global crises and local supply pressures shape food culture, influencing affordability, dietary choices, and everyday meals across the country

Producer Price vs Sentiment

The chart below shows us the changing producer price index within 2022 focusing mainly on common foods including Bananas, Cabbages, Eggs, Chicken, Duck and Okra on the left of the scale. On the right hand side is the average user sentiment score on Twitter regarding Food Security. The score scales from -0.99 to 0.99 where -0.99 has a completely negative sentiment and 0.99 is completely positive. We can notice a sharp drop between March to May. But overall sentiment has remained above 0.

Eating Out vs BMI

The graph below explores the relationship between the number of times a person eats out and their BMI levels. From the graph you can see that a majority of respondents have 3 meals a day with many of the responses clustering between the 20 to 30 BMI range.

Youth Food Frequency (Daily)

This bar chart shows us how frequently on average Respondants Age <= 18 who consume food items daily consume on a daily basis. For example, the respondants on average answers they eat chicken daily 3 times this means they eat chicken 3 times every day on average.

Youth Food Frequency (Weekly)

This bar chart shows us how frequently on average Respondants Age <= 18 who consume food items weekly consume on a weekly basis. For example, the respondants on average answers they eat noodles weekly 6 times this means they eat needles 3 times every week on average.

Youth Food Frequency (Monthly)

This bar chart shows us how frequently on average Respondants Age <= 18 who consume food items Monthly, consume on a Monthly basis. So for example, on average, a respondent that consumes rice monthly, will consume, 4 bowls a month.
